QoS General settings

CoS Scheduling mechanism - queue scheduling type:
  • State - permission to operate the QoS mechanism
  • Weighted fair priority - packets with different priority are processed by the switch with the ratio 8: 4: 2: 1. It means, the switch will process 8 high priority packets, 4 medium priority packets, 2 low priority packets and 1 packet with the lowest priority in one time unit.
  • Strict priority - packets with a higher priority will be processed first, until the high-priority packet queue becomes empty. Meanwhile, packets with lower priority will be in the queue.

Priority mode - priority for choosing CoS or ToS.
  • СoS only - only CoS is active and the package priority is determined only by the CoS field.
  • ToS only - only ToS is active and packet priority is determined only by the ToS field.
  • СoS & ToS - both CoS and ToS services are active, but if a packet containing both priority fields comes in, the priority is selected by the ToS field.


Default priority - riority by default. When the QoS mechanism is active and a packet that does not contain a priority field comes in, it is given the corresponding default priority by CoS and it is treated as a tagged packet with the appropriate priority.

Note 1. There are 8 CoS priorities. Priorities are numbered from 0 – lowest priority to 7 – highest priority.
